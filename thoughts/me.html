An outlook on our situation

No matter how much I think about existence, I always lands in some sort of absurdist position. Why am I writing this? Why did I wake up today and what the hell is the point of eating if we are going to die some day anyways when all eating does is delaying this inevitability. Any rational person would just have given up already, not neccesarily killing themselves because what is the point of killing yourself in the grand scheme of things anyways? But not doing anything else either, just kind of sitting on their ass until they starve to death or die of dehydration. So there seems to be two possible ways of dealing with this.

  • Firstly to just ignore how fucked up being alive is, this is how I assume most people do to survive each day.
  • The other option is to just accept it, not in the sense that you immediately starve to death because there's no point in eating. But rather just accepting that there is no rationale behind all the actions you take and chuckle to yourself over how dumb life the universe and everything actually is.

How I'd think about stuff

Having just acknowledged that I am one of those idiots that actually care about stuff without meaning, one might ask how. Well you kind of have to draw a line somewhere. When thinking about meanings of stuff without meaning, I kind of start on top with something like what is the point of me going out to run today or whatever. But that spawns even more questions in turn like why is running good, to stay healthy? What is the point of being healthy when when god could end me tomorrow for no particular reason. And why would I not go out for a run, there is not like there is more point to anything else. Also nobody cares if I do, but it's not like anyone else caring gives things meaning as those people are just as feeble and fleeting as yours truly. But at some point down the tree of questions posed you have to draw a line, like in the following chart. Level one is stuff we do and any deeper is further down the chain of abstractions although you could argue that we are more abstract the further up in the chart we are, doesn't matter much.

Level 1 Going to work/school Doing something fun Doing chores
Level 2 Caring what others think Caring about being healthy
Level 3 Eat when hungry Sleep when tired Idk pain not very good or smth
Level >= 4 Here be dragons

Going down to level 4 or beyond is not scary or something, it is just that everything becomes absurd and the things on level 3 is kind of where you have to stay to reach the conclusions above.

The things we ignore

But there are even more fundamental issues that any rational individual would solve before deciding to eat. Like what is the point of eating or drinking when we don't even know if we exist, sure feels like I exist but I could just be a boltzman brain floating out in space right now which invalidates all meaning to do things. Or how do I know anyone else is real, I mean most people look pretty much like a fellow human being. But I can't know now can I.

The thing is just that the people whot think like that would have gone extinct long ago. As natural selection does not take meaning of existence into consideration when deciding who to leave alive and not. And the ones who prioritise knowing why they exist before eating would definetly be the first to go, even though they are right. Leaving us featherless bipedsTM to continue constructing our concrete structures of existence on what might as well be a seabed that could sink at any moment.
